Description:

A monitor, also known as a computer display or screen, is an output device that visually presents information generated by a computer or other electronic device. It allows users to view text, images, videos, and graphical interfaces produced by the computer's graphics card or other video sources.

Monitors come in various sizes and technologies, including LCD (liquid crystal display), LED (light-emitting diode), OLED (organic light-emitting diode), and CRT (cathode ray tube) displays, although CRT monitors are less common today. LCD and LED monitors are the most prevalent types in modern computing.

keyboard

Description:

A keyboard is a peripheral input device used to enter text, numbers, and commands into a computer or other electronic device. It typically consists of a set of keys mounted on a flat surface, arranged in a specific layout, with each key representing a specific character or function.

The layout of physical keyboards varies depending on the region and language. The most commonly used layout is the QWERTY layout, named after the arrangement of the first six letters in the top-left row of keys. Other layouts, such as AZERTY and QWERTZ, are used in specific regions.

mouse

Description:

A mouse, in the context of computing, is an input device that allows users to interact with a computer by moving a cursor on the screen. It is typically a small handheld device that is moved across a flat surface, such as a desk or a mouse pad, and has one or more buttons for clicking and selecting items on the screen.

The standard mouse design consists of a plastic or metal casing housing various internal components. These components include a ball or an optical sensor for tracking the movement of the mouse, a mechanism to detect button clicks, and a cable or wireless technology for connecting the mouse to the computer.
